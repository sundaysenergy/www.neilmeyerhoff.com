Dead Vlei, Sossusvlei Sand Dunes

  • Namibia

Dead Vlei, a dry lake bed at the base of the Big Daddy sand dune contains trees that are thought to have been dead for over 500 years. This is a five image stitched panoramic image, combined in Lightroom. Olympus OMD, EM-1, 1/200@f16, 12mm, ISO 200

African Penguins, Boulders' Beach

  • South Africa

This is a two photo stitched panoramic image of African penguins at Boulders' Beach, part of the Table Mountain National Park. The park has developed a wooded walkway system to bring tourists closer to the penguins, but also to protect the penguins by limiting the area tourists can enter. Olympus OMD, EM-1, 1/320@f14, 40mm, ISO 200

Click image to enlarge.

THANGBI MANI FESTIVAL VI

  • Bhutan

During the first day of this teschu (religious festival) near Jakar in the Bumthang district in central Bhutan, it was raining and I was standing under the overhang of the temple. I turned around and noticed all these people were wedged into this one window watching the festival. A classic case of setting up a photograph, and then turning around 180 degrees to find a photograph. This image was taken with a Hasselblad X-pan camera.

Click image to enlarge.

FOX SHRINE AT FUSHIMI-INARI

  • Japan

Fushimi-Inari is a Shinto temple dedicated to the god of Rice, and many of the shrines that populate the grounds are dedicated to foxes, as shown here, as the foxes are the servants of the Rice God. This shrine is in the southern part of the city of Kyoto, Japan. This panoramic photograph was taken with a Hasselblad X-pan camera.

Click image to enlarge.

GUANAJUATO SEEN FROM FUNICULAR

  • Mexico

Guanajuanto, the capital city of the state of the same name, is built in the valley between two hillsides. There is a funicular which travels up the hillside to the south of the city, and from the top there are wonderful panoramic views of the city. This image was taken with a Hasselblad X-pan camera with a 45mm lens.

Click image to enlarge.

Bac Ha market

  • Vietnam

The Sunday market at Bac Ha is one of the most colorful events in the entire country of Vietnam, and photos of this event show up in most guide books about Vietnam. There are several different ethnic Hmong groups, but at the Bac Ha market, the Flower Hmong, the group to which these two women belong, predominate in this part of Lao Cai province.

Click image to enlarge.

These ornately dressed soldiers are standing in front of the Presidential Palace in the Plaza Grande, in the UNESCO World Heritage portion of Quito, Ecuador during the changing of the guard which occurs on most Mondays. This photograph was taken with a Hasseblad HD-31 camera.
