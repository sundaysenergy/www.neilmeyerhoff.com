OLD MAN WITH STAFF, JAKAR DZONG

  • Bhutan

This older man was visiting the Jakar dzong in the Bumthang district in central Bhutan. The dzongs are both administrative and religious centers and each district has a dzong. All the dzongs are architecturally similar, almsot all having originally been built within the reign of one leader in the 17th century. This vertical panoramic photograph was made with a Hasselblad X-pan camera.

Click image to enlarge.

View of Stradum

  • Croatia

The Stradum, also known as the Placa, was created in the 11th century when the inlet between two settlements silted in. Dubrovnik sits along the Dalmatian coast and is a favorite tourist destination in Croatia. This photograph was taken atop the western side of the city wall with a Hasselblad HD-31 camera.

CHILDREN VISITING THE GREAT BUDDHA

  • Japan

It seems that every Japanese school child at some point comes to Nara on a school trip. The children are all dress alike and have come visit the giant bronze buddha in the Todai-ji (Eastern Great Temple) at the former Japanese capital city of Nara. This image was made with a Hasselblad X-pan camera.

Click image to enlarge.

YOUNG INDIAN BOY

  • Mexico

On the first Friday of March, Indians from the area around San Miguel de Allende, Mexico, come to town to dance in celebration of the Feast of the Lord of the Conquest. The costumes are elaborate home made affairs, with many feathers and beaded clothing. This vertical panoramic photograph was created using a Hasselblad X-pan camera with a 45mm lens.
