Plaza San Francisco

  • Ecuador

The Plaza Dan Francisco is a major landmark in Quito, Ecuador, and is part of the UNESCO World Heritage site. This designation was created because the colonial part of Quito is one of the only extant Spanish towns from the colonial era. This photograph was taken with a Hasselblad HD-31 camera

Dubrovnik seen from city walls

  • Croatia

Dubrovnik is a medieval city protected by massive walls that surround the city to protect it from invaders from the sea. The city, the most popular tourist location in Croatia, sits along the Dalmatian coast. This photograph was taken from the northern side of the city wall with a Hasselblad HD-31 camera.

Matterhorn

  • Switzerland

The Matterhorn, at 4,478 meters is one of the symbols of Switzerland. The area nearest the base of the mountain is reached by a railway from the town of Zermatt. Clouds often conceal the mountain as they did later this day. This summer time photograph was taken with a Hasselblad HD-31 camera.

Click image to enlarge.

Herodes Atticus Theater, Acropolis

  • Greece

The Herodes Atticus Theater on the south side of the Acropolis in Athens, Greece is named after its builder. Built in 161CE, the theater is now the site of the summer Athens Festival. This photograph was taken with a Hasselblad HD-31 digital camera

Click image to enlarge.

Young Girl At School

  • Myanmar

As school let out for lunch, all the other children were in groups and joshing around with each other. This one girl was standing by herself with a contemplative look on her face. I circled around her taking about ten photos of her. I felt awkward approaching her, and unlike most of the photos I took of children in Myanmar, I kept my distance. She did, I think, understand I was taking her photo. Olympus OMD EM-5, 24mm, 1/250@f4.5.

Girl against red wall

  • India

I love the colors that are present in India, both in the clothes people wear and on the buildings as well. This girl was with a group of about six teenagers who were in Haridwar to bath in the Ganges during the Kumbh Mela festival that is held once every 12 years in Haridwar. I has taken several photos of the group and then asked them, by motioning since I don't speak Hindi, to let me take their individual photos against the red wall that was inside of the building at this particular bathing ghat. Haridwar, Uttarakhand. Nikon D700, 66mm, 1/160@f2.8
