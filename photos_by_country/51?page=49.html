American Flag, Broad Channel, #17

  • New York

An American flag stuck in a flower pot. In July 2009 my wife Sayra and I took a course at the International Center for Photography (ICP) which took us each day to a different part of New York. On July 27th, we went to Broad Channel in Queens, an area where many police and firemen live. I decided that every picture I took that day would have an American flag somewhere in the photo. I used a Panasonic Lumix, DMC-LX3 point and shoot that day as it looked like rain and I did not want to carry a heavier camera. I took 165 photos that day, and this image and those that follow are from that shoot.

Police Lined Up To Prevent Violence

  • Ecuador

The celebration of the solstice and harvest in June used to be a joyous occasion with religious overtones. However, in some small towns, like here at Cotacochi, the celebrations have deteriorated as neighborhood or village groups of men come to town very drunk, and march around with much noise, and sometimes try to engage with other inebriated groups of men. The police riot squads are present to keep the groups apart and on the expected parade route. This day I saw the police using their shields to push groups of men away from other groups. Olympus OMD EM-5, 12mm, 1/160@f5.6
