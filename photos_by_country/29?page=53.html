Padaung Tribal Woman With Brass Neck Rings

  • Myanmar

This Padaung tribal woman is one of a group who make their living weaving cloth for clothing and other purposes. We think the tradition of wearing neck rings is dying out, but in this house there were two teenage girls, 17 and 18, wearing neck rings as well. The girls said they wore them as a matter of tradition. While we as tourists might think they are being exploited by being on the lake making cloth for sale, they said it was much better than bending over all day in the fields with the brass rings around their necks. Olympus OMD EM-5, 24mm, 1/30@f3.2.

Click image to enlarge.

CHRISTIAN CHURCH FESTIVAL

  • India

These men with their drums and cymbals are part of the beginning of a celebration at this Christian church in the state of Kerala, India. This state has the highest percentage of Christians in India. The sweat on the men is a result of their exertions, but also the event is occurring at noon under the blazing sun of southern India.
